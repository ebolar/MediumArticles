## Parallels with renovating a legacy IT environment
<There are many parallels with renovating a legacy IT environment.> 
Legacy environments are typically a collection of channels, core systems, infrastructure and assorted workarounds.

These environments typically grew organically, either in response to gaps in existing solutions, or for low cost incremental delivery of changing requirements.  They typically have had multiple sponsors over the years and each has left a different fingerprint on the solution.  Vibe programming is changing the cost model for delivery - we are likely to see more of these environments in the future.

When to modernise, repair or replace
<When to do this vs modernise vs replace?>
  Replace is a complete transformation including product, process and operating model. (knockdown & rebuild, new development opportunity)
  Renovate/Modernise - rebuilding parts or all of the solution to make it more efficient (renovation)
  Repair
- At one end of town the approach is often to knockdown and rebuild, however we want to preserve the character of the place.
- Good bones - something to preserve vs too much broken stuff.
- How much money will we spend on maintenance (changes) vs renovation (upgrades) vs rebuild (system replacement and transformation). 


So how would we approach IT renovation in a landscape like this?

1. Security (keeping the dog in and getting rid of the poisonous plants)
  - First priority is security for the site.  Security Guardrails, Controls, tools and libraries
  - User Access Controls
  - Data Access Controls, RBAC, etc
  - Auditing/Logging, SIEM
2. Stability (roof, footings and drainage)
  - Infrastructure
  - Software versions
  - Controlled delivery & deployment process
  - Appropriate governance
3. Cleaning, Repairs and Maintenance (largely risk reduction)
  - Hazzard reduction - priority items (fixing the deck)
  - Repairs - removal of things that will cause you to spend money in future, or will degrade the value of what you have over time.  (Trees next to the house, uncovering the damp course)
  - Cleaning - removing things that get in the way and are no longer needed (removal of toil, efficiency).  (The old peg board and trophy shelf in the garage)
  - Preventive Maintenance (Painting)

These are examples of technical debt.  <alignment to strategy is in the next section>

4. Renovations where it is most needed (when do you really need a new kitchen or bathroom)
  - Places where the cost of incremental fixes exceeds the cost to replace
  - Core capabilities to invest in that simplify the environment and make it easier to extend or change.
  - When business transformation is required
  - To add value
<these are the true strategic plays - must be cost justified>
